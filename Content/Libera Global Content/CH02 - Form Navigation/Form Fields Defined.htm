Form Fields Defined

Buttons


Examples of buttons that could be included on a form.

 

Some forms use buttons to provide more information about a specific field in a new window, or to open another form.

To use a button, single-click it or tab to it and press ENTER or SPACEBAR.

 

 Check Boxes


Check Boxes in a form.

 

Check boxes are commonly used when there is a list of options where the user may select one or multiple options.

To select or clear a check box, click it with your mouse or TAB to the check box field and then press the SPACEBAR.

 

 Editable Regions


Example of a letter with editable regions.

 

Some forms, most often letters, allow you to edit the text of certain paragraphs or areas on the form. Editable Regions are not true fields and do not store the data changes to a table. Instead the changes are saved with the form.

Click into the Editable Region or tab to it and then use the arrow keys to position the cursor to the area to be edited. Make the changes and then close the form. Changes save automatically.

 

 Memo Fields


Example of a memo field.

 

Memo Fields allow an unlimited number of words. To fill out a memo field, click the mouse with the cursor inside the field or press the TAB key on your keyboard to reach the field and begin typing.

Formatting options are available for text entered in a Memo Field so users may format text as desired.

Note: When you are working in a memo field you may open the Extended Editor for text formatting ease. You may access the Extended Editor off of the Edit menu or by pressing CTRL+ENTER when your cursor is in the memo field.

 

 Text Fields


Example of a text field.

 

Text fields limit the number of characters you can enter. To fill out a text field, click or TAB to the field and begin typing. Text fields may only allow letters or numbers.

 

 Text Field with Lookup



Example of a lookup field.

 

Many text fields have lookups (also known as picklists). Lookups give you a list of options. Lookup fields have a blue down arrow at the right end of the field. To use a lookup, follow these steps:

  1. Single-click the blue down arrow or press the ALT+F1 keys on your keyboard. A new window opens showing options to select from in a grid.
  2. Double-click on an option in the grid, or press the ENTER key on your keyboard with that option selected. This action closes the lookup window and populates the field with your selection.

Note: Some lookups will fill data into multiple fields, such as the Zip lookup. It will usually fill in the City, State, and County (if applicable) in addition to the selected ZIP.

 

 Formatted Text Fields


Example of formatted text fields.

 

Some Text Fields are pre-formatted allowing you to enter data without needing to type dashes, slashes, or parenthesis.

To fill out a formatted text field, click or tab to the field and begin typing.

 

  • To quickly fill out today’s date in a date field, type the letter T and tab out of the field. Think T for Today!
  • To quickly fill out yesterday’s date in a date field, type the letter Y and tab out of the field. Y for Yesterday!
  • To quickly fill out tomorrow's date in a date field, type the letter R and tab out of the field.
  • If you are entering a date within the current year, you may type only the MM/DD and then TAB out of the field. The current year will automatically fill in.
  • You may enter dates in the MM/DD/YY format, they will automatically change to MM/DD/YYYY.

 

 Yes/No/Blank


Example of Y/N/B fields.

 

Yes/No/Blank or fields are used with Y/N questions. The only allowable responses are a Y for Yes, an N for No, or blank to leave the field empty.

You may use the mouse or the SPACEBAR to cycle through Yes, No, or blank. Additionally, you may type a Y, or an N, or leave the field blank.
