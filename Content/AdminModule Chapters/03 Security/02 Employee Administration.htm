Employee Administration.

 

Edit Mode: Form and Grid

Note: While you may edit data directly in the grid, it's easier to edit data via the Employee Information form.

Description: The Employee Administration option allows Administrators to track employees within an organization as well as other system users. This aids with both human resource records and establishing system security. Administrators can add new employees, terminate employees, change roles for employees with new job responsibilities, enter outside users as an employee (in order to assign them a user name and password), change the office assigned to the employee, and assign an employee to a caseload or team that has already been created.

The Employee Administration options displays a list of all currently active employees who have access to the system. An active employee is defined as an employee without a termination date in the past. Employees with termination dates of today or in the future will still appear in this table.

Employees with termination dates before today appear in the Terminated Employees option under Group: Security within System Administration. Selecting an employee record in the table opens the employee's Employee Information form.

 

Adding a New Employee to the System
  1. Select the Action Row where it says, "Please click here to add a new record..." This opens a new Employee Information form.
  2. Fill out the form as you would any other form.

 

 Editing Existing Employee Data
  1. Select the Employee in the grid to open his/her Employee Information form.
  2. Edit fields on the form as you would with any other form.

 

 Terminating an Employee

Terminating an employee prevents the employee from accessing the system, removes them from the Employee Administration table, removes them from lists in the system, and ends any of their caseload, team, or worker assignments.

  1. Select the employee to be terminated in the grid. This opens their Employee Information form.
  2. Go to the Termination Date field and enter the date of termination.
  3. Close the form.
