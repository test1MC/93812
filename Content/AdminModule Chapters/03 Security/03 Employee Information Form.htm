Employee Information Form


Top portion of the Employee Information form.

 

The Employee Information form is used to add employees/users to the system. Use this form to maintain employee data, caseload and team assignments, and user rights within the system.

 

 

Employee Data

Much of data in this section pulls into other forms in the system or affects what data the employee may see.

  • Worker ID - This is a unique identification "number" that appears in the client's Electronic Case Folder (ECF) and depending on your Agency's application settings in Case Notes. You may enter up to three alpha or numerical characters. This data is required when assigning employees to caseloads and teams.
  • Employee Name and Title - This information is often pulled into many different forms in the application. The employee's title does not designate their user role in the application.
  • Termination Date - When an employee is terminated enter the date terminated here. You don't want to delete them from the application because their name and ID numbers are needed for historic reference.
  • Office - This field is required. It links the employee to the office they work in which gives them access to the appropriate budget accounts and client data. Also associates them with a district/region. When the employee changes offices, update this field with the new office.
  • Supervisor - Required. There is built in functionality within the system that calls to this information. For example a form that the employee opens and fills out may require a supervisor signature. The supervisor's name comes from this field. When an employee gets a new supervisor, update this field accordingly.
  • Phone, Fax, Cell, TTY, Email - Depending on your Agency's settings some or all of this information may flow into form headers, form letters, and in signature sections of various forms.
  • Team to assign when adding a new client (If empty, employee will be assigned) - Optional. When clients are added to the system, the employee who adds them automatically appears as a worker assignment in the case assignment section of the Demographic Information form. Selecting a team from the lookup/picklist for this field, overrides the automatic worker assignment. Instead, the selected team automatically appears in the case assignment section of the Demographic Information form.

 

 Authorization Settings


Authorization setting section of the Employee Information form.

Note: Completing this section is optional.

  • Authorization Level - This applies to the employee who writes the authorization. When used, it limits the maximum dollar amount the employee can authorize per authorization. To use this field, type up to eight numeric characters to represent a whole dollar amount. When you tab out of the field the dollar sign, commas, and decimal point appear automatically. Should the authorization amount be over the employee's limit, an additional signature line for the employee's supervisor is added to the authorization form.
  • Increase Authorizations - This applies to the employee who writes the authorization. After the authorization is approved...

    ... A N (No) response means that employee cannot go over the original approved amount.

    ... A Y (Yes) or if the field is left blank, response means that they can go over by the percentage specified in the % Authorization Increase field.
  • Require original signator to approve changes - When marked Y (Yes) and the employee makes changes to an authorization after it is approved and those changes would cause an additional signature line to be added, this setting causes an additional signature line for the user who originally signed the authorization instead of the supervisor of the user that made the change.
  • Program - Allows you to set authorization limits by program for the employee. All of the fields in the Authorization Limits section are related to the program selected. If a program is not selected, the other fields are applied to all programs. Note: You may set authorization limits for multiple programs and Account Identifiers.
  • Account Identifier - Field opens for editing once a Program is selected. Allows you to specify an Account Identifier for the selected program. This affects the account selection process on Authorization forms.
  • % Authorization Increase - This applies to the employee who writes the authorization. Use this in conjunction with the Increase Authorizations field. Note: if the response to Increase Authorizations is No, entering a value into this field will not change anything. Type a numeric value between .01 and 1000 for the percent.

    After the authorization is approved and the employee is allowed to increase the authorization amount, the employee may increase the amount of the authorization by the percent entered.

    For example, if the original approved amount of the authorization was $1,000.00, and the employee's increase authorization percentage is 10%, the maximum amount the authorization could be increased to is $1,100.00.
  • Amount Authorization Increase - You may allow authorization increases by percentage OR specific amount, not both. When you limit by amount, the employee may only increase authorizations up to the amount entered and only if the response to Increase Authorizations? is Y (Yes).
  • Required Role - When filled in, an additional signature line appears on authorization forms for users with this role instead of the employee's direct supervisor when the employee reaches his/her authorization limits.
  • Yearly Authorization Limit - This applies to the employee requesting the authorization. When used, it limits the maximum dollar amount the employee can authorize per year. To use this field, type up to eight numeric characters to represent a whole dollar amount. When you tab out of the field the dollar sign, commas, and decimal point appear automatically. Employees will not be able to write any authorizations when their yearly authorization limit is met.

 

 User Rates


User Rates section of the Employee Information form.

 

Note: Completing this section is optional.

User Rates are specifically tied to fiscal progress notes of any progress report form for any program. If the note is written by a vendor, then the pricing tiers are open to select from in the progress report form. If the note is written by an employee, that is not a vendor providing that service, the rates pull from the user rates entered.

You may add as many user rate sections as needed. Enter user rates if this employee provides services for the agency. The fields in this section allow you to specify the rate and the account the funds should come out of based on the values entered for program, account identifier, and special project.

 

 System Notifications


System Notifications section of the Employee Information form.

Note: Completing this section is optional.

The Available System Notifications section varies depending on which system notifications used by your agency. This is controlled within the System Notifications option in the System Administration module. Only active notifications appear in the Employee Information form.

You can sign an employee up to receive any of the available notifications by responding Y (Yes) to the Subscribe? field for notification. This causes their email address to automatically populate the Send To field. To stop sending a notification, change the response to N (No) and clear the Send To field.

Notifications send a system generated email to the employee any time the criteria of a notification is met.

 

 User Authentication Information


User Authentication Information section of the Employee Information form.

This is where you give employees log on credentials to the system.

If your Agency uses Windows Authentication or an External Authentication mechanism, so employees don't have to enter a user name and password every time they use the system, make the Username equal to their network log on. There is no need to create a password for employees in this instance. Optionally, if you allow employees to use the system remotely, outside of a VPN, you may enter a password. This would be used when using the system outside of your internal network.

If your Agency doesn't use Windows Authentication or an External Authentication mechanism, you must create a Username and Password for employees. They will use this Username and Password every time they want to use the system.

You may set rules for passwords with Application Settings within the System Administration module. When applied, you won't be allowed to enter passwords that don't meet the criteria you set.

 

 Employee Roles


Employee Roles section of the Employee Information form.

Employee Roles are what gives employees access to various forms, PDQs, reports, tools, and other functionality within the system. You may add as many roles as necessary to employees.

Primary Role? determines which Default PDQs load when the employee selects a module from the Application Home Page.

 

 Team Administration


Team Administration section of the Employee Information form.

Note: Completing this section is optional if your Agency won't be using Caseload or Team Assignments. Otherwise, it should be completed.

This section shows the Team and Caseload Assignments for the employee. They have the same fields and work the same way.

  • Caseload/Team – The lookup options are grouped first by the office, then by caseload/team. Expand the grouped rows to see the employees assigned to the caseload/team. Selecting an employee selects the caseload/team. This action closes the lookup and populates the Caseload/Team field with your selection.
  • Start Date – This is the date the assignment started or will start. Clearing the Start Date clears the Primary flag (if selected).
  • End Date – This is the date that the assignment ended or will end.
    • If you specify an End Date that is before today, the Primary option is locked after an amount of time set by your Agency.
    • If blank, this field remains open for editing after the other fields lock. It will lock after a date is entered.
  • Primary? – Press SPACEBAR to select the assignment as the primary assignment. A Y (Yes) indicates that it is selected.
    • If a caseload/team does not have a primary counselor designated, you may not select it as the Primary caseload for a client.
    • Changing the Primary selection on historic assignments will not affect current assignments.

 

 Delegate Information


Delegate Information section of the Employee Information form.

Note: Completing this section is optional.

Delegates are employees that can fill in for another employee should that employee be unavailable for an extended period of time. Usually, delegates will have the same or greater rights within the system. You may add as many delegates as necessary. You may add and remove delegates as necessary.
